Exploration & European Influence

Explorers brought European knowledge to Arizona, dramatically changing the culture and lifestyle of the Native Americans.

After the Europeans began to have a greater influence on the Western Hemisphere, Native Americans were affected quite drastically.

Through trade, Native Americans were able to acquire new crops to plant and take advantage of, along with animals such as horses to help them in travel and hunting.

The Native Americans were able to learn a lot from the Europeans as well. They learned about language, religion, culture, mathematics, and more.

While they benefitted from European Influence, they were negatively impacted as well. Disease was brought to the Americas, causing a very large majority of Native Americans to die, as they were not prepared for the outbreak.




Many Native Americans were also very upset with European influence. The rise in deaths following European arrival and the mistreatment by the American Colonists caused many tribes to rebel against Europeans. While they fought hard, they were almost completely unsuccessful in being respected like they wanted by Europeans.

Luckily, in the future, the United States Government finally began to recognize the mistreatment of Native Americans, giving them designated Native American Reservations to live on and more rights and respect in the country.

Arizona has one of the largest Native American populations out of all 50 states, as well as having a huge portion of land designated for Reservations. Reservations in Arizona are home to tribes such as the Navajos and Apaches.

The majority of Reservations are in Northern Arizona, in relative isolation from the populous cities such as Phoenix, Tucson and Flagstaff.
