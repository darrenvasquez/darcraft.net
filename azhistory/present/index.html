Present Day Arizona




With a population of over 6.5 million as of 2014, Arizona has become one of the most rapidly developing states in the United States.

Arizona is known worldwide for the beautiful Grand Canyon, a massive canyon located in northern Arizona. Millions of tourists visit this natural wonder each year to explore the beauty of Arizona.

Arizona is also known for it's beautiful desert landscape in places such as Sedona and vast mountain ranges. These areas attract hikers, campers and hunters to explore and experience the state.

The state of Arizona continues to grow today, becoming a major tourist attraction and home to the lovers of hot summers and mild winters.



Fun Facts About Arizona

· Arizona's nickname is the Grand Canyon State.

· Navajo Indians from Arizona were recruited to the U.S. Marines after the Japanese bombed Pearl Harbor in 1941. Known as Navajo Code Talkers, the Navajo men created a code that the Japanese was unable to understand, fulfilling a crucial role during World War II and saving countless lives.

· Arizona is only one of two states that does not observe Daylight Savings Time.

· Arizona's flag contains a copper-colored star that represents the fact that Arizona is one of the leading producers of copper in the country.

· Phoenix Metropolitan Area has a population of about 4.4 million, making it the 14th most populated metropolitian area in the United States.

· The city of Phoenix has a population of 1.5 million, making it the 6th most populated city in the US. It also is the 10th largest city in the United States with an area of 516 mi²
