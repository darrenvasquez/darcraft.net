Typical Manors

Typical Manors consisted of many different buildings and farms for many different purposes. There was a church for worshipping, cottages for the serfs to live in, mills for grinding grains, a castle or luxurious house for the Lord, and much more. There were also many pastures for animals to be raised for meat, milk, and other resources. Fields were also very complex, usually using a three-field system farming method. This was a method where they would switch off between fields, usually between seasons. This allowed the fields to "regenerate" their nutrients to allow crops to be grown well. Each manor relied on everyone to keep everything working well defended, supplied, and more.
